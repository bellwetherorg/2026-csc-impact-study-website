How to Get Involved

CSC makes participating in the IST study simple and easy. Here’s what the process looks like for schools, from interest to implementation.

What Schools Are Asked to Do

Participating schools commit to a focused set of actions that CSC supports throughout:

  1. Designate a point of contact: One staff member (often an assistant principal, literacy coach, or instructional coordinator) serves as the primary liaison with CSC staff.
  2. Sign a Data Sharing Agreement (DSA): A standard agreement that allows CSC and researchers to access existing student roster and assessment data. This follows all applicable federal and state privacy laws.
  3. Identify 50 or more eligible students: Work with CSC to determine which students in grades 4–8 would benefit most from literacy tutoring.
  4. Schedule tutoring time during the Design Sprint: Carve out approximately 90 minutes per week of tutoring time during the school day, for a minimum of 10 weeks. CSC facilitates and supports this planning.
  5. Share a student roster: Before tutoring begins, share the list of students receiving tutoring.
  6. Implement HIT: Implement 90 minutes a week of tutoring for at least 10 weeks for identified students.
  7. Allow observations: Allow CSC to monitor tutoring via two observations a year.
  8. Share interim assessment data: If your LEA cannot share interim literacy assessment data (NWEA MAP, i-Ready, etc.), schools will be expected to share participating students’ interim assessment data at the end of the school year.
  9. Encourage staff, students, and families to complete surveys: Annual feedback surveys help CSC continuously improve its programming.
  10. Participate in brief research activities: Some schools may be asked to participate in site visits or interviews with staff, families, and leaders to help researchers understand how implementation is working.

CSC staff and the Bellwether research team work with your team throughout this process.
